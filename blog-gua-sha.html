Gua Sha Changed My Evening Routine—Here's How to Do It Right

I'll be honest—I was skeptical about gua sha at first. It looked like just another beauty trend, and I wasn't sure how scraping my face with a stone would help. But after trying it for a month, I'm hooked. Not only does it help me relax, but my skin has never looked better. Let me teach you how to do it right.

What Even IS Gua Sha? The Ancient Practice That Changed My Skincare Routine

Gua sha is an ancient Chinese practice that dates back over 2,000 years. The name comes from the Chinese words "gua" (to scrape) and "sha" (redness)—but don't let that scare you! When done correctly, it's not about creating redness; it's about gentle stimulation. You use a smooth tool (usually made of jade, rose quartz, or obsidian) to glide across your skin in specific patterns.

Here's how it works: The gentle scraping motion improves blood circulation and stimulates lymphatic drainage. Your lymphatic system is like your body's "garbage disposal"—it removes toxins and excess fluid. When it's sluggish, you get puffiness and dull skin. Gua sha helps kickstart that process.

I'll admit, I was totally skeptical at first. I saw videos of people scraping their faces and thought, "That looks painful!" But then I tried it. The first time I did it, I felt this amazing release of tension in my jaw and forehead. It was like getting a deep tissue massage for my face. And the next morning? My skin looked brighter, less puffy, and more lifted. I was hooked.

Don't confuse gua sha with "facial scraping" that leaves marks—that's not what we're doing here. Proper gua sha is gentle, relaxing, and should never hurt. Think of it as a form of self-care that happens to have amazing skin benefits.

What You'll Need to Get Started

You don't need much—just a few simple things:

  • Gua sha tool: I recommend jade or rose quartz—they're smooth and cool on the skin. The BAIMEI set is my favorite.
  • Facial oil or serum: This is crucial! You need something to help the tool glide. I use jojoba oil or my favorite moisturizing serum.
  • Clean skin: Always start with a clean face—no makeup!

My Step-by-Step Gua Sha Routine

I do this 2-3 times a week, usually at night before bed. It's become my favorite part of my skincare routine.

Step 1: Prep Your Skin

Cleanse your face thoroughly, then apply a generous amount of oil or serum. You want enough product so the tool glides easily—dry skin will tug and irritate.

Step 2: Start with Your Neck

Hold the gua sha tool at a 15-45 degree angle. Start at your collarbone and glide upward along your neck towards your chin. Do this 5-10 times. This helps drain lymphatic fluid and reduce neck tension.

Step 3: Jawline and Cheeks

Move to your jawline—start at your chin and sweep along the jaw towards your ears. Then move to your cheeks: start near your nose and sweep outward towards your temples. Remember—always move upward and outward, following your facial contours.

Step 4: Forehead

Start in the center of your forehead and sweep outward towards your hairline. This helps reduce tension headaches and smooths out fine lines.

Step 5: Under Eyes (Optional)

Be super gentle here! Use the small edge of the tool and glide from the inner corner of your eye outward. This helps reduce puffiness and dark circles.

Why I Love Gua Sha (And Why You Will Too)

Here are the benefits I've noticed:

  • Less puffiness: My face used to be so puffy in the morning. Now? I look awake even before my coffee!
  • Glowing skin: The increased blood flow makes my skin look bright and healthy.
  • Relaxation: It's like a mini spa day at home. The gentle scraping is incredibly calming.
  • Better product absorption: My serums sink in better after gua sha.

My Top Tips for Beginners

  • Start gentle: You don't need to press hard! The goal is to stimulate, not scrape.
  • Use enough oil: Dry tool = irritation. Trust me on this.
  • Clean your tool: Wash it with soap and water after each use to prevent bacteria.
  • Be consistent: I saw results after about 2 weeks of regular use.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Pressing too hard: You're not trying to exfoliate—you're trying to massage.
  • Moving downward: Always go upward to fight gravity and reduce sagging.
  • Using on broken skin: Wait until acne or cuts are healed.
  • Skimping on oil: Dry skin + dry tool = irritation. Always use enough product!
  • Doing it too often: 2-3 times a week is plenty. Overdoing it can cause redness.

Common Questions About Gua Sha (FAQ)

Q: Does gua sha really work?

A: Yes! When done correctly, it improves circulation, reduces puffiness, and relieves tension. I've seen visible results in my skin texture and tone after just 2 weeks of regular use.

Q: Is gua sha painful?

A: Not at all! If done gently, it should feel relaxing, not painful. You might feel a slight "tingling" sensation from the increased blood flow, but that's normal.

Q: How often should I do gua sha?

A: I do it 2-3 times a week at night. Overdoing it can cause irritation, so listen to your skin.

Q: Can gua sha help with acne?

A: It can help with inflammation and redness, but avoid using it directly on active breakouts. Focus on the areas around your acne instead.

Q: What's the best time to do gua sha?

A: Nighttime is ideal—it becomes part of your wind-down routine. Some people do it in the morning to reduce puffiness before work.

My Gua Sha Tool Maintenance Routine

To keep your gua sha tool in great shape:

  • Clean after each use: Wash with warm, soapy water to remove oil and bacteria.
  • Disinfect weekly: Soak in rubbing alcohol for 5 minutes, then rinse and dry.
  • Store properly: Keep it in a soft pouch or on a clean surface—avoid dropping it!
  • Replace when needed: If the edges become chipped or dull, it's time for a new tool.
