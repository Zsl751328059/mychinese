Cookie Policy

Last updated: January 1, 2026

This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how Healthy Living uses them on our website, and your choices regarding cookies.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They help websites remember your preferences, understand how you use the site, and improve your overall browsing experience. Cookies are widely used across the internet and are essential for many website features to function properly.

Types of Cookies We Use

We use the following types of cookies on our website:

Cookie Type Purpose Duration
Essential Cookies Required for the website to function properly. They enable core features like page navigation, secure areas access, and shopping cart functionality (if applicable). Session / Persistent
Analytics Cookies Help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. This helps us improve our website's functionality and content. Persistent
Functionality Cookies Allow the website to remember choices you make (such as your language preference or region) and provide enhanced, personalized features. Persistent
Advertising Cookies Used to display relevant advertisements based on your interests. These may be set by our advertising partners and help us offset website costs. Persistent
Affiliate Cookies Persistent

Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies are placed by third-party services that appear on our pages. We use the following third-party services that may set cookies:

  • Google Analytics: To analyze website traffic and user behavior. You can opt out of Google Analytics by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.
  • Google AdSense: To display relevant advertisements based on your browsing history and preferences.

How Long Do Cookies Last?

Cookies can be either "session cookies" or "persistent cookies":

  • Session Cookies: These are deleted automatically when you close your browser. They help the website function during your current visit.
  • Persistent Cookies: These remain on your device for a set period (ranging from days to years) and are activated each time you revisit the website. They help remember your preferences across multiple visits.

Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You have several options for managing cookies:

Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • View what cookies are stored on your device
  • Delete all or specific cookies
  • Block cookies from all or certain websites
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Clear all cookies when you close the browser

To manage cookies in your browser, visit the help pages for:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

Online Preference Tools

You can also manage your preferences for certain advertising cookies through tools like:

  • Digital Advertising Alliance Consumer Choice Page
  • European Interactive Digital Advertising Alliance

Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you choose to disable cookies, please note that some parts of our website may not function properly:

  • Some features may not work as intended
  • Your preferences may not be saved for future visits
  • You may see more generic advertisements instead of relevant ones
  • Affiliate tracking may be affected, potentially impacting commission earnings
