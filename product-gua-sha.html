Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about gua sha

How often should I use the gua sha tool?

+

For best results, use the gua sha tool 3-5 times per week. You can use it daily if your skin tolerates it well. Consistency is key for seeing results.

What kind of oil should I use with gua sha?

+

Use a facial oil or serum that absorbs well into the skin. Facial oils like jojoba, rosehip, or argan oil work best. Avoid using gua sha on dry skin as it can cause friction.

Is jade gua sha better than other materials?

+

Jade is a traditional choice for gua sha due to its cooling properties and smooth surface. It stays cool to the touch, which helps reduce inflammation and puffiness. Other materials like rose quartz also work well.

How do I clean my gua sha tool?

+

Clean your gua sha after each use with warm water and mild soap. Pat dry with a soft cloth. Store it in a clean, dry place to prevent bacteria growth.

Can gua sha help with wrinkles?

+

While gua sha won't eliminate deep wrinkles, regular use can help improve blood circulation, reduce puffiness, and promote a more lifted, youthful appearance.

Is gua sha safe for sensitive skin?

+

Yes, gua sha is generally safe for sensitive skin when used gently. Start with light pressure and shorter sessions. If you have active acne or rosacea, consult with a dermatologist before using.
