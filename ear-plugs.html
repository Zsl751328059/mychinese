Ear Plugs Buying Guide

Everything you need to know before buying ear plugs

🎧

Noise Reduction Rating (NRR)

Look for NRR ratings between 20-33dB. Higher ratings mean more noise blocked.

🛌

Comfort for Sleeping

Choose soft materials like foam or silicone. Contoured designs work best for side sleepers.

♻️

Reusability

Reusable plugs save money and reduce waste. Look for washable materials.

🔊

Hearing Protection

For concerts or loud environments, choose high-fidelity plugs that preserve sound quality.

FAQs About Ear Plugs

For sleeping, soft, moldable ear plugs made from silicone or foam are ideal. Look for a Noise Reduction Rating (NRR) of 25-32 dB for optimal noise blocking.

Reusable ear plugs are more eco-friendly and cost-effective over time, while disposable ones are convenient for travel and single-use situations.

When used correctly, ear plugs are safe. Avoid inserting them too deeply and clean reusable plugs regularly to prevent irritation or infection.

For general use, 20-25 dB is sufficient. For loud environments like concerts or construction sites, look for 28-32 dB ratings.
